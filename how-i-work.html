How I work

Built around your business, not a package.

There's no standard program here. We start by understanding where the business actually stands, then build the strategy and structure it needs to run and grow. Most owners start one of three ways and grow into another.

The engagement, step by step

From a clear look at today to a business that runs on its own.

01

Assess

A clear-eyed look at where the business actually stands. The strengths, the gaps, and the things quietly holding it back.

02

Set the direction

Vision, values, and the priorities that matter, broken into 90-day goals your leadership team understands and owns.

03

Build the system

The meetings, the numbers, the processes, and the accountability that turn the plan into how the company runs every week.

04

Hand it over

I run it with your team, then teach them to run it themselves, until it's theirs. Then I'm back for quarterly planning, not every week.

Three ways to work together

Pick the door that fits where you are.

01

Business Operating Framework

The core engagement. The full arc above: assess, set the strategy, build the structure it runs on, and stay hands-on until your team owns it.

  • A clear-eyed assessment of where things stand today
  • Vision, direction, and 90-day priorities your team owns
  • The weekly and quarterly meeting rhythm
  • Real data that gives you visibility into the health of your business
  • Documented processes that actually get followed
  • Hands-on facilitation, then a handover to your team
02

Owner & Executive Coaching

A sounding board for the people who make the hard calls. A natural place to start if you're not ready for the full framework.

  • Regular one-on-one sessions on your cadence
  • The real problem, your options, a way forward
  • Leadership and decisions under pressure
  • An honest outside view, with no agenda
  • A lower-commitment way to begin
03

Fractional Leadership

An experienced hand in a leadership seat, part-time, for as long as it helps.

  • GM, operations, technology, or help managing projects like ERP implementations
  • Part-time, short or long term
  • Steady leadership without a full-time hire
  • Senior-level experience without the full-time cost
What gets stronger

Good structure builds five things into a business.

Each builds on the next, working together to create a business that's easier to run and less chaotic. It shifts from relying on one person to relying on the process, grows steadily and deliberately, and is more profitable and more valuable on an exit.

1

Clarity

A real direction, built from your vision and broken into priorities the team understands and owns. People make good calls without waiting on you.

2

Visibility

The few numbers that show how the business is truly doing, in front of the people who need them. Decisions on facts, not gut.

3

Communication

A structured way information moves, from the leadership team down through every team, so everyone's working from the same picture.

4

Accountability

Everyone arrives knowing what was expected and what the numbers say they delivered. No hiding, no blame. I hold you to it too.

5

Consistency

Documented processes that reach every part of the business and actually get followed, so it performs predictably, whoever's in the room.

What working together is like

In the room with you, then out of your way.

I'm not there to hand you a binder and leave. Early on I'm in the room every week, running the meetings and teaching your team to run them. We do the real work together, and we adjust as the business tells us what it needs.

The goal is never for you to need me forever. It's a business that runs on structure instead of on heroics, a leadership team that owns the week, and an owner who gets to lead again instead of firefight. When that's in place, I step back to quarterly planning and the occasional honest conversation.
