You'vegot $10,000 and a dream.  You start abusiness and find early success.  You'regreat at what you do and build a good reputation, and the business rolls in.  It starts to become a bit much for just you,so you hire someone to help.  Then youwake up one day with an office, 7 employees, 50 customers, and things arefalling apart.  Your customers areunhappy, your employees are unhappy, you can't keep track of everything, youhave no processes, no structure, and cash flies in and out of your bank accountlike food in your golden retriever's breakfast bowl.  You're working crazy hours, can't find timeto grow the business, can't fix the problems you know about and are terrifiedof the ones you don't.  Gut feel isn't workinganymore.

 

This is ashift many new business owners struggle to make.  You're great at doing "the work",but when you reach this point it's no longer enough.  You have to learn to stop working IN yourbusiness and start working ON it.  It's ahard leap to make - maybe harder than starting it in the first place.  You're going to need expertise in operations,sales, marketing, accounting, procurement, legal, HR, IT.  If you're lucky you know one or two of thosebut you'll need help with the rest.  

 

Your jobnow is to pull yourself away from the day to day and focus on the biggerpicture.  Create the structure, makeplans, execute on the plans, make decisions based on data and facts, not onentirely on gut feel (there is always some value to good instincts).  You have to learn to hire, train, and rely onother people - people who may do things a bit different than you did, and maybeeven better. 

 

Thehardest part about this journey is that you have to grow through a phase whereyou will develop the need for a lot of different functions, but can't afford orjustify the cost of a person dedicated to that function.  You slowly take hats off yourself and putthem on someone else, rely on contract or outsourced help until you can justifythe dedicated roles.  Then, one dayyou'll wake up with a business with 35 employees, 200 customers, and all yourplanning and hard work is paying off.
